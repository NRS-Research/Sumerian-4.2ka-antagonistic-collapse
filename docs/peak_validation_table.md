# Validation of ~2190 BCE Aridity Peak
This table summarizes multi-proxy evidence supporting the definition of ~2190 BCE as the peak of the 4.2 ka climate event in Mesopotamia, corresponding to Table S1 in the Supplementary Information.

| Proxy / Record Type | Original Time Window (ka BP) | Converted Time Window (BCE) | Key Evidence | Corresponding Reference |
|---------------------|------------------------------|------------------------------|--------------|--------------------------|
| Gol-e-Zard Cave Mg/Ca (speleothem dust proxy) | 4.26 – 3.97 (high plateau across mid-interval) | 2310 – 2020 (peak plateau centered at ~2190) | Abrupt rise to sustained high dust flux; maximum values occur in the central 100-year segment of the event | Carolin et al. (2019) [1] |
| Gol-e-Zard Cave δ¹⁸O (speleothem aridity proxy) | 4.2 – 4.1 (maximum enrichment) | 2250 – 2150 (peak centered at ~2200) | Gradual increase to maximum isotopic enrichment, indicating strongest regional drying | Carolin et al. (2019) [1] |
| Gulf of Oman deep-sea eolian dust record | 4.1 ± 0.1 (peak dust deposition) | 2250 – 2150 | 5-fold increase in Mesopotamia-sourced dolomite dust; peak within dating error of 2190 BCE | Cullen et al. (2000) [2] |

## Notes
1. All three independent records place the maximum aridity/dust intensity within the 2250–2150 BCE window, and ~2190 BCE falls at the center of this overlapping range.
2. Chronological discrepancies between records are within the published dating uncertainties (±30 to ±70 years, 1σ).
3. The ~2190 BCE peak is a derived synthesis conclusion from multiple proxies, not a single explicitly labeled date from any individual source paper.

## References

[2] Cullen, H. M. et al. Climate change and the collapse of the Akkadian empire: Evidence from the deep sea. *Geology* 28, 379–382 (2000).
