# Detailed Conflict Events: 2150–2101 BCE

### Detailed Conflict Events: 2150–2101 BCE (Midpoint: 2125 BCE)

*All events are formal inter-polity military actions between sovereign Sumerian city-states, compiled from royal inscriptions, administrative records and chronological syntheses. Dating follows the Middle Chronology framework, with all events falling within the 2150–2101 BCE 50-year bin per the chronological binning protocol.*

### Notes

1. **Chronological placement**: All events are assigned to the 2150–2101 BCE bin based on their median date under the Middle Chronology. Events with ±30-year uncertainty are placed in the bin containing their median date, consistent with the binning protocol defined in Supplementary Section S2.2.3.
2. **Scope alignment**: Events 1–6 are core Lagash-Umma border conflict episodes (the primary empirical anchor of the antagonism index), directly documented in royal inscriptions compiled by Cooper (1983). Events 7–14 are contemporaneous inter-city conflicts across the southern Sumerian plain, synthesized from the regional political chronology by Liverani (2014).
3. **Weighting rule**: All 14 events are counted with equal weight (one event = one count). No magnitude scaling is applied, as quantitative data on troop sizes or casualties are unavailable and unreliable for this period.
